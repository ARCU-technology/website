Kinokroonika CSN

Explore Estonian Newsreels with the Kinokroonika CSN

The Estonian Newsreels were a series of short news documentaries that captured the life and events of Estonia from 1923 to 1997. They are a valuable source of historical and cultural information, but they are also difficult to access and explore due to their large volume and inherent complexities of the medium. With the Kinokroonika CSN, we address this challenge and provide an interactive visualization interface that allows users to navigate the film footage of the Estonian Newsreels in an explorative way.

https://csn.kinokroonika.ee

Processing the data

Utilizing automatic shot detection (Castellano, 2014/2023), we divided the newsreel video material into 165 thousand segments and selected the middle frame from each shot to represent its footage scene in a single image. We then extracted visual features from these images, using a ResNet50 (He et al., 2016) model, a Convolutional Neural Network trained on the ImageNet (Deng et al., 2009) dataset. The resulting feature vectors, essentially digital fingerprints, comprised a multidimensional space in 2048 dimensions.

To visualize this multidimensional vector space in familiar 2D, we utilized Dimensionality Reduction techniques such as UMAP (McInnes et al., 2018) and t-SNE (Maaten & Hinton, 2008). These so-called projections serve as visual interpretations of the multidimensional space, in which images with shared visual features appear closer together. While these projections are useful for visual examination and exploration, they are only an approximation of the underlying multidimensional space. Therefore, we should be careful not to over-interpret the results (cf. Chari & Pachter, 2023).

Using the Kinokroonika CSN

The Collection Space Navigator (CSN) (Ohm et al., 2023) is an open-source visualization interface, primarily developed to explore large visual collections and their multidimensional representations. The CSN is used here with parts of the collection of the digitized Estonian Newsreels from the National Archives of Estonia, contributing to the Kinokroonika project.

Users can zoom in and out, navigate through the content, and apply filters based on specific criteria such as year, issue, and shot number. This functionality enables users to pinpoint specific historic timelines and aspects of interest within the collection. Additionally, the filtered data can be conveniently downloaded either as metadata in a CSV file or as a PNG image of the visual space.

The interface incorporates sliders on the right-hand side, providing control over the range of each metadata filter and presenting the distribution through interactive histograms. Clicking on a histogram allows users to glide through issues, years, or shot numbers by swiping over the bars, revealing all the objects they encompass.

The subsets

For the Kinokroonika CSN web version, we divided the collection into digestible subsets. On the top right-hand side, users can choose between:
1938-1941: the time right before the first Soviet occupation of Estonia and when it started;
1944-1949: the beginning of the second Soviet occupation of Estonia;
1963-1965: the gradual emergence of new film culture, as well as political changes of Brezhnev replacing Khrushchev in 1964;
1973-1976: the mature Soviet period;
1985-1989: the political and cultural changes leading towards Estonia’s independence and the shift from the Nõukogude Eesti (Soviet Estonia) newsreel series to Eesti Kroonika (Estonian Chronicle) in 1988.

Each subset presents distinct projections of the data. “UMAP” and “t-SNE” showcase a visual similarity space, illustrating how similar images cluster together. “Issue Distribution” offers a yearly breakdown of the issues, providing an overview of their distribution. Meanwhile, “Storyboard” organizes all shots in chronological order, offering a narrative structure to the newsreel.

Full research version

Two versions of the Kinokroonika CSN are available: a web-optimized version, which provides insight into the data with interesting subsets, and the full research version, which operates offline. The local research version includes the entire dataset of 164,902 shots from 1923 to 1997, with additional features such as search queries. It requires a powerful computer and at least 50GB of free disk space. Contact us if you are interested in running the Kinokroonika CSN research version on your hardware.
